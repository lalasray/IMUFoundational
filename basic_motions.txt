ANATOMICAL MOTION PRIMITIVE SET
(Six body-part groups with primitives, pace, and intensity)

BODY PART GROUPS
1. Head
2. Torso
3. Left Arm
4. Right Arm
5. Left Leg
6. Right Leg

PRINCIPLES
1. Use anatomical motion primitives rather than activity words.
2. Represent complex actions as combinations of primitives over time.
3. Keep pace and intensity separate from the primitive itself.
4. Use hold/static as the zero-motion state.

Head (7 primitives):
1. flexion (bend forward / look down)
2. extension (bend backward / look up)
3. lateral flexion left (tilt left)
4. lateral flexion right (tilt right)
5. axial rotation left (turn left)
6. axial rotation right (turn right)
7. hold/static (keep still)

Torso (10 primitives):
1. flexion (bend forward)
2. extension (bend backward / arch)
3. lateral flexion left (lean left)
4. lateral flexion right (lean right)
5. axial rotation left (twist left)
6. axial rotation right (twist right)
7. anterior tilt (tilt forward)
8. posterior tilt (tilt backward)
9. translation / shift (move body position without rotation)
10. hold/static (keep still)

Left Arm (14 primitives):
1. shoulder flexion (raise arm forward / overhead)
2. shoulder extension (move arm backward)
3. shoulder abduction (raise arm out to the side)
4. shoulder adduction (bring arm back toward the body)
5. shoulder internal rotation (rotate arm inward)
6. shoulder external rotation (rotate arm outward)
7. elbow flexion (bend elbow)
8. elbow extension (straighten elbow)
9. forearm pronation (turn palm down)
10. forearm supination (turn palm up)
11. wrist flexion (bend wrist forward)
12. wrist extension (bend wrist backward)
13. wrist deviation (tilt wrist side to side)
14. hold/static (keep still)

Right Arm (14 primitives):
1. shoulder flexion (raise arm forward / overhead)
2. shoulder extension (move arm backward)
3. shoulder abduction (raise arm out to the side)
4. shoulder adduction (bring arm back toward the body)
5. shoulder internal rotation (rotate arm inward)
6. shoulder external rotation (rotate arm outward)
7. elbow flexion (bend elbow)
8. elbow extension (straighten elbow)
9. forearm pronation (turn palm down)
10. forearm supination (turn palm up)
11. wrist flexion (bend wrist forward)
12. wrist extension (bend wrist backward)
13. wrist deviation (tilt wrist side to side)
14. hold/static (keep still)

Left Leg (13 primitives):
1. hip flexion (lift thigh / leg forward)
2. hip extension (move leg backward)
3. hip abduction (move leg out to the side)
4. hip adduction (bring leg toward the midline)
5. hip internal rotation (turn leg inward)
6. hip external rotation (turn leg outward)
7. knee flexion (bend knee)
8. knee extension (straighten knee)
9. ankle dorsiflexion (lift foot upward)
10. ankle plantarflexion (point foot downward)
11. ankle inversion (roll foot inward)
12. ankle eversion (roll foot outward)
13. hold/static (keep still)

Right Leg (13 primitives):
1. hip flexion (lift thigh / leg forward)
2. hip extension (move leg backward)
3. hip abduction (move leg out to the side)
4. hip adduction (bring leg toward the midline)
5. hip internal rotation (turn leg inward)
6. hip external rotation (turn leg outward)
7. knee flexion (bend knee)
8. knee extension (straighten knee)
9. ankle dorsiflexion (lift foot upward)
10. ankle plantarflexion (point foot downward)
11. ankle inversion (roll foot inward)
12. ankle eversion (roll foot outward)
13. hold/static (keep still)

PACE OPTIONS:
- Static: no movement
- Slow: slow or relaxed motion
- Fast: quick or rapid motion

INTENSITY OPTIONS:
- Low: small amplitude, gentle effort
- High: large amplitude, forceful effort

OPTIONAL TEMPORAL MODIFIERS
- single
- repeated
- alternating
- oscillatory
- asymmetric
- synchronized
- sequential
- abrupt
- smooth

COMPOSITE MOTION EXAMPLES
Example: "Walking"
- Head: flexion + extension (slow pace, low intensity)
- Torso: axial rotation left + axial rotation right + lateral flexion left + lateral flexion right (slow pace, low intensity)
- Left Arm: shoulder extension + shoulder flexion + elbow flexion + elbow extension (slow pace, low intensity)
- Right Arm: shoulder extension + shoulder flexion + elbow flexion + elbow extension (slow pace, low intensity)
- Left Leg: hip flexion + hip extension + knee flexion + knee extension + ankle plantarflexion + ankle dorsiflexion (slow pace, high intensity)
- Right Leg: hip flexion + hip extension + knee flexion + knee extension + ankle plantarflexion + ankle dorsiflexion (slow pace, high intensity)

Example: "Reaching overhead with left hand" - Variant 1
- Head: hold/static
- Torso: extension (slow pace, low intensity)
- Left Arm: shoulder flexion + elbow extension + wrist extension (slow pace, high intensity)
- Right Arm: hold/static
- Left Leg: hold/static
- Right Leg: hold/static

Example: "Reaching overhead with left hand" - Variant 2
- Head: hold/static
- Torso: lateral flexion left (slow pace, low intensity)
- Left Arm: shoulder abduction + elbow extension + wrist deviation (slow pace, high intensity)
- Right Arm: hold/static
- Left Leg: hold/static
- Right Leg: hold/static

Example: "Reaching overhead with left hand" - Variant 3
- Head: hold/static
- Torso: lateral flexion right (slow pace, low intensity)
- Left Arm: shoulder flexion + elbow extension + wrist deviation (slow pace, high intensity)
- Right Arm: hold/static
- Left Leg: hold/static
- Right Leg: hold/static

NOTES
- Circumduction is not a primitive; it is a coordinated sequence of flexion/extension and abduction/adduction, often with rotation.
- Swing is not a primitive; it is a time-varying pattern built from one or more primitives.
- Nod, shake, tilt, twist, rock, bounce, and thrust are composite patterns, not primitive motions.
