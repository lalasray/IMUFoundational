Possible Pace Categories for Motions

1. Static
   - No measurable movement; held posture or stillness.
   - Example: head hold, seated posture, arms at sides.

2. Occasional
   - Infrequent adjustments or movements; less than 1 Hz.
   - Example: quick glance, occasional weight shift.

3. Slow
   - Movements at roughly 0.1–0.5 Hz (one motion every 2–10 seconds).
   - Example: deliberate bend, slow head turn, gentle arm swing.

4. Medium
   - Movements at roughly 0.5–1.5 Hz (one motion every 0.7–2 seconds).
   - Example: walking gait, moderate torso sway, typing wrist flexion.

5. Fast
   - Movements at roughly 1.5–3 Hz (several cycles per second).
   - Example: jogging leg motions, rapid wrist flicks, dribbling.

6. Very Fast / Vigorous
   - Movements above ~3 Hz or high amplitude rapid actions.
   - Example: running sprint, aggressive arm swings in sports, clapping quickly.

Each pace label can be tailored to the sensor sampling rate or application requirement.  Use these descriptors when annotating basic motions or activities to convey speed/intensity.
